1. Overview: What is HSBC Life Wealth Accelerate?

HSBC Life Wealth Accelerate is a whole life, regular premium Investment-Linked Plan (ILP). This means it's a financial product that combines life insurance protection with investment opportunities, aiming to grow your wealth over the long term.

You commit to paying regular premiums for a chosen Minimum Investment Period (MIP), which can be either 25 years or 30 years. You can also choose to pay your premiums in SGD or USD (though monthly USD payments are not allowed).

A key feature is that it's a Guaranteed Issuance Offer, meaning acceptance is generally guaranteed without medical underwriting, making it accessible.

The plan is designed to reward long-term commitment through various bonuses while providing flexibility and protection. However, like all investments, it carries risks, and the value of your investment can go up or down.

2. How Your Money Works: IUA & AUA

This plan uses two main accounts to manage your premiums and investments. Understanding these is key to seeing how your money grows and how fees are applied.

Initial Units Account (IUA)

Think of this as your "starting pot."

  • Your Regular Premiums go into the IUA during the Initial Contribution Period (ICP).
  • For a 25-year MIP, the ICP is the first 4 years (48 months).
  • For a 30-year MIP, the ICP is the first 5 years (60 months).
  • All Start-up Bonuses are credited here.
  • The Account Maintenance Fee (AMF) is charged on the value of this account during the MIP.
  • If you surrender the policy early (during MIP), the Early Encashment Charge (EEC) is based on the IUA value.

Accumulation Units Account (AUA)

This is your "growth pot" for the longer term.

  • Your Regular Premiums go into the AUA *after* the Initial Contribution Period (ICP) ends.
  • Any Top-up Premiums or Recurring Single Premiums (RSP) you make go directly here.
  • Power-up Bonuses and Loyalty Bonuses are credited here.
  • The Investment Management Fee (IMF) is charged on the value of this account.
  • Partial Withdrawals are typically made from the AUA.

Both accounts hold units in the ILP Sub-Funds you choose to invest in. The value of these units can rise and fall based on market performance.

3. Supercharge Your Growth: Bonuses

HSBC Life Wealth Accelerate offers several bonuses designed to boost your investment value over time. These are credited as additional units into your policy.

Start-up Bonus

Get a head start! This bonus is paid on each Regular Premium received during the first few years of your policy and is credited to your IUA.

For 25-Year MIP:

  • Year 1: 25% of Regular Premium
  • Years 2-4: 40% of Regular Premium each year
  • Total Start-up Bonus: 145% of one annual Regular Premium

For 30-Year MIP:

  • Year 1: 30% of Regular Premium
  • Years 2-4: 40% of Regular Premium each year
  • Year 5: 50% of Regular Premium
  • Total Start-up Bonus: 200% of one annual Regular Premium

Note: Start-up Bonus may not be payable in certain situations like early death by suicide, non-accidental death/terminal illness within 18 months of policy start/reinstatement, or policy cancellation during the free-look period.

Power-up Bonus

A reward for staying invested! This bonus is paid monthly starting from the first policy month of Policy Year 15 until the end of your MIP. It's calculated on your prevailing Total Account Value and credited to your AUA.

For 25-Year MIP (Years 15-25):

  • Annual Premium < S$9,600/US$6,720: 1.00% p.a.
  • Annual Premium ≥ S$9,600/US$6,720: 1.05% p.a.

For 30-Year MIP (Years 15-30):

  • Annual Premium < S$9,600/US$6,720: 1.25% p.a.
  • Annual Premium ≥ S$9,600/US$6,720: 1.30% p.a.

Eligibility: Power-up Bonus may be suspended for 12 months if you make a Partial Withdrawal from AUA, take a Premium Holiday, or reduce your Regular Premium.

Loyalty Bonus

Thank you for your long-term commitment! This bonus is paid monthly *after* your MIP ends, for the rest of your policy term. It's calculated on your prevailing Total Account Value and credited to your AUA.

After 25-Year MIP (From Year 26 onwards):

0.90% p.a.

After 30-Year MIP (From Year 31 onwards):

1.10% p.a.

Eligibility: Loyalty Bonus may be suspended for 12 months if you make a Partial Withdrawal.

4. Understanding the Costs: Fees & Charges

It's important to be aware of the fees and charges associated with this plan, as they will affect your overall returns. These rates are guaranteed unless stated otherwise.

Account Maintenance Fee (AMF)

Charged monthly from your IUA during the MIP.

Rate: 3.4% per annum of the IUA value.

This fee stops after the MIP.

Investment Management Fee (IMF)

Charged monthly from your AUA throughout the policy term.

Rate: 1.0% per annum of the AUA value.

Early Encashment Charge (EEC)

Applies if you surrender the policy or if it lapses due to unpaid premiums during the ICP, or if the premium holiday period is exceeded during MIP and premiums remain unpaid. This charge is a percentage of your IUA value.

The EEC rate reduces each year. For example:

  • Year 1: 100%
  • Year 5: 91%
  • Year 10: 82%
  • Year 15: 56%
  • Year 20: 23%
  • Year 25: 8%
  • Year 26 onwards: 0%
  • Year 1: 100%
  • Year 5: 96%
  • Year 10: 91%
  • Year 15: 58%
  • Year 20: 24%
  • Year 25: 17%
  • Year 30: 8%
  • Year 31 onwards: 0%

Refer to Appendix A of the Product Summary for the full EEC rate table.

Partial Withdrawal Charge (PWC)

Applies to each partial withdrawal made from the AUA during the MIP, unless it's a Free Partial Withdrawal.

Rate: 7% of the partial withdrawal amount.

No PWC applies for withdrawals made after the end of the MIP.

Bonus Recovery Charge (BRC)

Imposed if you reduce your Regular Premium amount during the MIP. This charge is to recover a portion of the Start-up Bonus you received.

It's deducted from the IUA when the reduced premium takes effect.

Formula: `Start-up Bonus received x (1 - (Number of months passed since policy inception / Committed MIP in months)) x % of Premium reduction`

Fund Management Charges

These are charged by the respective fund managers of the ILP Sub-Funds you choose. They are not directly deducted from your policy but are reflected in the unit price of the funds. These charges vary from fund to fund and are not guaranteed.

Switching Fee & Redemption Fee

Currently waived by HSBC Life. However, they reserve the right to impose these fees in the future.

5. Your Safety Net: Policy Benefits

Beyond investment growth, this plan provides important insurance protection.

Death Benefit

If the Life Assured passes away while the policy is in force (excluding suicide within the first year or first year of reinstatement), the following will be paid:

  • Death before Policy Anniversary nearest to Life Assured's 66th birthday:
    • 101% of (Total Account Value)
    • PLUS 15% of (Total Account Value - Top-ups - RSPs), capped at S$500,000 / US$350,000
    • Less any outstanding fees and charges.
  • Death on or after Policy Anniversary nearest to Life Assured's 66th birthday:
    • 101% of (Total Account Value)
    • Less any outstanding fees and charges.

Note: If non-accidental death occurs within 18 months from commencement/reinstatement, the Death Benefit will not include the Account Value relating to the Start-up Bonus.

Terminal Illness (TI) Benefit

If the Life Assured is diagnosed with a Terminal Illness while the policy is in force, a lump sum will be paid as an advancement of the Death Benefit. The amount is the lower of:

  • SGD 3 million (or equivalent)
  • The Death Benefit payable at the point of TI claim.

The maximum TI Benefit across all policies with HSBC Life and other insurers for the same Life Assured is SGD 3 million. If TI condition commences within 18 months from commencement/reinstatement, the TI Benefit will not include the Account Value relating to the Start-up Bonus. The policy may terminate or continue with a reduced value depending on the payout.

Life Replacement Option (LRO)

A unique feature allowing an individual policyholder to request to change the Life Assured on the policy. Subject to conditions, such as:

  • Policyholder must be an individual.
  • Proof of insurable interest on the new Life Assured.
  • New Life Assured must meet eligibility criteria (age, alive).
  • New Life Assured can be spouse, child (below 18), or the policyholder themselves (if originally a third-party policy).
  • New riders may require underwriting. Insurance Charge will be based on the new Life Assured.
  • Previous assignments or nominations must be revoked.
  • Waiting periods for benefits will apply to the new Life Assured.
  • Any attached Riders on the original Life Assured will be dropped.

HSBC Life reserves the right to approve or reject the LRO request.

Dividend Distribution (for dividend-paying ILP Sub-Funds)

If you invest in ILP Sub-Funds that pay dividends, you can choose to:

  • Reinvest dividends: Buys more units in the fund, increasing your policy value. This is the default.
  • Receive cash payout: Paid to your designated bank account if the dividend amount is S$30 or more. Otherwise, it will be reinvested.

Dividend distributions are not guaranteed and depend on the fund manager.

6. Flexibility for Life's Changes

Life is unpredictable. This plan offers several options to adapt to your changing needs.

Partial Withdrawals

You can withdraw part of your account value, subject to conditions. Minimum withdrawal is S$1,000 / US$700.

During ICP (Initial Contribution Period):

  • From AUA only (if you've made top-ups).
  • Subject to 7% PWC.
  • Withdrawal from IUA is not allowed.

After ICP to End of MIP:

  • From AUA only.
  • Free Partial Withdrawal Benefit:
    • Policy Years 1-15 (after ICP): 2 free withdrawals total.
    • Policy Years 16-20: 1 additional (max 3 total, less previous).
    • Policy Years 21-25: 2 additional (max 5 total, less previous).
    • Policy Years 1-15 (after ICP): 2 free withdrawals total.
    • Policy Years 16-20: 2 additional (max 4 total, less previous).
    • Policy Years 21-25: 1 additional (max 5 total, less previous).
    • Policy Years 26-30: 2 additional (max 7 total, less previous).
     Each free withdrawal is capped at 2 times your Annualised Regular Premium.
  • Other withdrawals subject to 7% PWC.
  • *Impact:* No Power-up Bonus for 12 months after *any* partial withdrawal.
  • Withdrawal from IUA is not allowed.

After End of MIP:

  • From IUA or AUA.
  • No PWC.
  • *Impact:* No Loyalty Bonus for 12 months after *any* partial withdrawal.
  • Remaining Total Account Value must meet Minimum Account Balance (S$3,600/US$2,520).
Premium Holiday

Allows you to temporarily stop paying Regular Premiums.

  • Availability:
    • Not allowed during ICP. Policy will lapse if premiums unpaid.
    • Allowed after ICP until end of MIP: Up to a total aggregate of 84 months.
    • Allowed after end of MIP: Unlimited duration.
  • Activation: Can be applied for, or triggers automatically if Regular Premium is unpaid after grace period (provided Total Account Value is sufficient to cover charges).
  • Impact during MIP:
    • No Power-up Bonus during the holiday or for 12 months after resuming payments (unless missed premiums are fully repaid under specific conditions).
    • Policy charges (AMF, IMF) continue to be deducted from Total Account Value.
    • If Total Account Value becomes insufficient to cover charges, premium holiday ceases, and you'll be asked to resume payments. Failure to do so can lead to policy lapse and EEC.
  • Repayment of Missed Premiums: Possible under specific conditions, which can reinstate missed Power-up/Loyalty Bonuses.
Reduction of Regular Premium

You can apply to reduce your Regular Premium amount.

  • Availability: Any time after ICP until end of Policy Term.
  • Limit: Can be reduced by a maximum of 25% of the original Regular Premium, subject to minimum premium requirements.
  • Consequences during MIP:
    • Bonus Recovery Charge (BRC) applies (deducted from IUA).
    • No Power-up Bonus for 12 months.
  • Once reduced, you cannot increase it thereafter.
Increase of Regular Premium

The Regular Premium set at policy inception cannot be increased at any time.

Recurring Single Premium (RSP) & Top-Up Premium

You can make additional ad-hoc investments into your policy.

  • Allowed anytime during the policy term (except during Premium Holiday).
  • Allocated to AUA. No premium charge on these.
  • Minimum RSP: S$100 per month (multiples of S$100). Not allowed for USD.
  • Minimum Top-up Premium: S$5,000 / US$3,500 (multiples of S$100/US$100).

7. Is This Plan for You? Suitability & Key Risks

Who is it Suitable For?

  • Individuals seeking a long-term investment and savings solution (MIP of 25 or 30 years).
  • Those comfortable with investment risks for potentially higher returns compared to traditional savings.
  • People who appreciate a disciplined approach to regular premium payments.
  • Those who want life insurance coverage integrated with their investments.
  • Individuals attracted by bonus features that can enhance wealth accumulation.
  • Singaporeans planning for long-term goals like retirement or general wealth building, who also value protection.

Unique Selling Points (USPs)

  • Triple Bonus Structure: Start-up, Power-up, and Loyalty bonuses.
  • Life Replacement Option: Flexibility to change the Life Assured.
  • Guaranteed Issuance Offer: Generally no medical underwriting required.
  • Choice of SGD or USD policy currency.
  • Protected under the Policy Owners' Protection Scheme in Singapore.

Key Risks & Important Notes

  • Investment Risk: The value of your ILP Sub-Funds can fall as well as rise. You may lose the principal amount invested. Past performance is not indicative of future results.
  • Long-Term Commitment: Early termination or surrender, especially during the MIP, usually involves high costs (like EEC) and the surrender value may be less than total premiums paid.
  • Fees and Charges: These will impact your overall returns. Understand them fully.
  • Market Fluctuations: Economic and market conditions can affect fund performance.
  • Non-Guaranteed Benefits: Bonuses (except Start-up Bonus rates, if premiums are paid) and fund returns are not guaranteed. Power-up and Loyalty bonuses depend on prevailing account value and meeting eligibility.
  • Policy Terms & Conditions: This is a summary. The actual terms are in the Policy Contract and General Provisions.
  • Free-Look Period: You have 14 days from receiving your policy to review it. If you cancel within this period, premiums paid will be refunded less any medical/other expenses incurred by the insurer. The refund amount for ILPs can be affected by market movements.

Always consult a qualified Financial Planner to assess if this product is suitable for your specific financial situation, objectives, and risk appetite before making a commitment.
