1. Product Overview

HSBC Life Wealth Voyage is a whole life, regular premium Investment-Linked Plan (ILP). It's designed to provide insurance protection up to Age 99 while allowing your money to be invested in a range of ILP Sub-Funds to meet your investment needs. You select a Minimum Investment Period (MIP) of 15, 20, or 25 years.

This product is available in SGD and USD. The value of your investments can go up or down, and buying a life insurance policy is a long-term commitment. Early termination usually involves high costs.

Key Objective: To provide a combination of insurance protection and investment growth potential over a long-term horizon, with various bonuses to reward commitment.

2. Account Structure

HSBC Life Wealth Voyage uses two main accounts for premium allocation and investment management:

Regular Premium Account

  • Regular Premiums paid by you are allocated here.
  • Start-up Bonuses, Power-up Bonuses, and Loyalty Bonuses are credited here.
  • Subject to Account Maintenance Fee (AMF), Premium Holiday Charge (PHC), Partial Withdrawal Charge (PWC), and Early Encashment Charge (EEC) under specific conditions.

Top-up Account

  • Any Top-up Premiums you make are allocated here.
  • Subject to a 3% premium charge upon allocation.
  • Withdrawals from this account are generally not subject to PWC or EEC (but check specific conditions for full surrender after partial withdrawal).
  • Not eligible for Start-up, Power-up, or Loyalty Bonuses.

4. Fees and Charges

Understanding the fees is crucial as they impact your investment returns. Most charges are deducted by cancellation of units.

Premium Charge

- Regular Premiums: No premium charge (100% allocated to purchase units).
- Top-up Premiums: 3% of the Top-up Premium paid (deducted after allocation). Guaranteed.

Premium Holiday Charge (PHC)

Charged monthly if Regular Premiums are unpaid during MIP (after the first 24 policy months and outside the 24-month Maximum Free Premium Holiday Duration). Ceases upon resumption of premium payment. Deducted from Regular Premium Account.

Monthly PHC = (Applicable PHC rate x Annualised Regular Premium) / 12.

PHC rates vary by Policy Year and MIP (e.g., MIP 15, Year 3: 74%; MIP 20, Year 3: 98%; MIP 25, Year 3: 85%). Rates generally decrease over time. Refer to product summary for full table. Not guaranteed.

PHC is waived if the premium holiday falls within the Maximum Free Premium Holiday Duration of 24 months (applicable from 25th policy month during MIP).

Account Maintenance Fee (AMF)

Charged monthly from Regular Premium Account. Payable throughout policy term, even during premium holiday. Not guaranteed.

Annual AMF Rate:

  • During 15-Year MIP: 2.30% p.a.
  • During 20-Year MIP: 2.15% p.a.
  • During 25-Year MIP: 2.15% p.a.
  • After MIP: 1.00% p.a. (for all MIPs)

Monthly AMF Calculation (General):
`(Annual AMF rate / 12) x [Higher of (Initial ARP and Current ARP)] x [Lower of (Policy Year and selected MIP)]`

Special Calculation for last years of MIP:

  • MIP 15 (last 3 policy years): Monthly AMF = (2.30% / 12) x [Higher of ARP] x 12
  • MIP 20 (last 4 policy years): Monthly AMF = (2.15% / 12) x [Higher of ARP] x 16
  • MIP 25 (last 6 policy years): Monthly AMF = (2.15% / 12) x [Higher of ARP] x 19

"Higher of ARP" refers to the higher of annualised Regular Premium at Commencement Date and prevailing annualised Regular Premium.

Bonus Recovery Charge (BRC)

Charged if Regular Premium is reduced during MIP. Deducted from Regular Premium Account. Not guaranteed.

Formula: `[Current Start-up Bonus $ - Reduced Start-up Bonus $] x (1 - (Months Premium Paid / Committed MIP in months))`

Partial Withdrawal Charge (PWC)

Imposed on withdrawals from Regular Premium Account during MIP. Deducted from withdrawal amount. Rates vary by Policy Year and MIP (e.g., Year 3: 50% for all MIPs; Year 15: 5% for all MIPs). Not guaranteed. Not applicable to Top-up Account withdrawals or after MIP.

Refer to product summary for full PWC rate table. No PWC for withdrawals from Top-up Account or after MIP from Regular Premium Account.

Early Encashment Charge (EEC)

Imposed on Regular Premium Account if policy is fully surrendered or terminates (other than by death/TI) during MIP. EEC rates are guaranteed and reduce annually.

EEC does not apply to Top-up Account. Additional EEC may apply if full surrender occurs within 24 months of a partial withdrawal from Regular Premium Account.

Other Charges

- **Switching Fee:** Currently Nil. HSBC Life reserves the right to impose.
- **Redemption Fee:** Currently Nil. HSBC Life reserves the right to impose.
- **ILP Sub-Fund Annual Management Fee & Other Charges:** Charged by fund managers, vary by fund, reflected in unit price. Not guaranteed.

5. Insurance Protection & Benefits

Death & Terminal Illness (TI) Benefit

Sum of:
(i) Higher of [101% of total Regular Premiums paid (less cumulative partial/regular withdrawals from Regular Premium Account)] OR [Account Value of Regular Premium Account]
(ii) PLUS Account Value of Top-up Account.

TI diagnosis must be >12 months prognosis, confirmed by specialist. Max TI benefit S$3M aggregate. Exclusions apply (e.g., TI in presence of AIDS/HIV).

Accidental Death Benefit

If death by Accident before Age 75, pays sum of:
(i) Higher of [200% of total Regular Premiums paid (capped S$2M or equivalent), less cumulative partial/regular withdrawals from Regular Premium Account] OR [Account Value of Regular Premium Account]
(ii) PLUS Account Value of Top-up Account.

If Death Benefit is higher, Death Benefit is paid. Exclusions apply.

Life Replacement Option (LRO)

Request to replace Life Assured up to 3 times, subject to conditions (insurable interest, underwriting, etc.). Original riders terminate. New waiting periods apply.

Maturity Benefit

At Policy Expiry Date (Age 99), pays 100% of Total Account Value, less outstanding charges/amounts.

6. Plan Flexibility

Premium Payments

Minimum Annual Regular Premium: S$3,600 / US$3,600. Monthly option for SGD (S$300).

Changes to Regular Premium:

  • 15-Year MIP: Allowed from 5th Policy Year onwards.
  • 20-Year MIP: Allowed from 8th Policy Year onwards.
  • 25-Year MIP: Allowed from 10th Policy Year onwards.

Reduction: Max 25% of original. BRC applies during MIP. No Power-up Bonus for 12 months if reduced during MIP.

Increase: No back-payment of Start-up Bonus for increased portion.

Premium Holiday

Temporarily stop Regular Premiums.

  • First 24 Policy Months: Not allowed. Policy lapses if premium unpaid (EEC applies).
  • From 25th Policy Month to end of MIP: Allowed.
    • Max. 24 months Free Premium Holiday Duration (PHC waived).
    • Beyond this, applicable Premium Holiday Charge (PHC) applies.
    • Power-up Bonuses forfeited during holiday (can be reinstated if missed premiums are back-paid under conditions).
  • After MIP: Allowed. No PHC.
  • Policy remains in force if Regular Premium Account value covers charges.
Top-up Premiums

Allowed from 13th Policy Month, before Life Assured Age 70. Min. $5,000 (multiples of $10), Policy Currency. Capped at 2x original annualised Regular Premium over policy term. 3% premium charge. Allocated to Top-up Account. Not allowed during Premium Holiday.

Withdrawal of Units

Partial Withdrawal:

  • Allowed from 25th Policy Month.
  • Min. amount $250 (multiples of $10).
  • Remaining Regular Premium Account value must meet Minimum Holding Amount (18 months' Regular Premium).
  • Withdrawals from Regular Premium Account during MIP subject to PWC (varies by year/MIP).
  • No PWC for Top-up Account withdrawals.
  • Impacts Power-up/Loyalty Bonus eligibility for 12 months.

Regular Withdrawal:

  • Allowed after MIP. Min. annual S$1,200/US$1,200 (or S$100 monthly).
  • Remaining Regular Premium Account value must meet Minimum Holding Amount.
  • Impacts Loyalty Bonus eligibility for 12 months.

Full Surrender: Possible anytime. EEC applies to Regular Premium Account if surrendered during MIP.

Switching of ILP Sub-Funds

Allowed. Min. switch amount $1,000 per fund, min. remaining $1,000 per fund. Max 10 funds. No switching fee currently.

Dividend Distribution

For dividend-paying funds: Option to reinvest (default) or receive cash payout (min. S$30 total from both accounts, else reinvested). Payouts in SGD.

7. Suitability and Key Risks

This Plan May Suit You If:

  • You seek long-term wealth accumulation (15, 20, or 25 years MIP).
  • You want combined investment and insurance protection.
  • You are comfortable with investment risks for potential higher returns.
  • You can commit to regular premium payments.
  • You value features like bonuses and some flexibility.

Key Risks to Consider:

  • Investment Risk: Value of units can fall; principal loss is possible. Returns are not guaranteed.
  • Long-Term Commitment: Early termination incurs high costs (EEC, PWC) and may result in value less than premiums paid.
  • Fees & Charges: These reduce overall returns. AMF, PHC, PWC, and fund charges are not guaranteed and can be revised by HSBC Life with notice.
  • Bonus Conditions: Power-up and Loyalty bonuses are subject to eligibility and can be suspended.
  • Policy Terms: This is a summary. The Policy General Provisions are binding.
